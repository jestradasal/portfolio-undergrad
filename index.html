Judyth Estrada

Hi! I'm Judyth. I'm a junior at Duke University pursuing an interdepartmental major between Computer Science and Sociology.

My technical interests include web development, machine learning and databases. I have had internships and other experiences where I have been able to explore these interests. Although I am now an interdepartmental major, I am still interested in working in full technical roles after graduation.

The other side of my interests include social justice, inequities existent within our society as well as different ways that technology can alleviate them. I first became interested in the humanistic side of tech when I read an article on the use of algorithms to decide sentencing, and how these algorithms would disproportionately affect people of color. Although these areas are wide, one field that I am most interested in is inequity in education and how technology can be harnessed to help fix it.

One of the ways that I am exploring this is through my Bass Connections team this year - CSbyUs. Founded by three undergraduate Duke students, we develop a Computer Science curriculum, teach it at various Durham middle schools and develop an open-source friendly web platform for teachers, students and others to use freely. The team believes that Computer Science education is an effective way to benefit disadvantaged students.

Other areas that interest me include the medical field and its intersection with sociology and technology. Some of my favorite books include Survival of the Sickest, The Hate U Give and Being Mortal.
